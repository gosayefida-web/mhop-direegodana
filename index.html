Metabolic Health Optimisation Project

At Diree Godana Teaching Hospital, we believe that metabolic health is not a clinical problem to be managed — it is a human story to be understood.

Metabolic Health Optimisation goes beyond managing blood sugar or controlling weight. It is about restoring your body's natural ability to produce, use, and regulate energy — efficiently, reliably, and sustainably. It is not about restriction or endless monitoring. It is about understanding how your body works and giving it exactly what it needs to work better — through the right nutrition, the right movement, the right behaviour, and the right clinical support at the right time.

Our approach is rooted in science but applied with care for the whole person — your life, your culture, your circumstances, and your goals. Because metabolic health does not exist only in a clinic. It exists in every meal, every step, every night of sleep, and every choice made across a lifetime.

The Metabolic Health Optimisation Project at Diree Godana is built on one conviction: that every patient deserves not just to live longer — but to live stronger.

About the programme

A preventive medicine pathway for metabolic disease

The Metabolic Health Optimisation Project (MHOP) at Diree Godana Teaching Hospital was established to provide a structured clinical pathway for patients at risk of, or living with, Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus. The programme operates across three defined clinical stages — prediabetes, established T2DM, and advanced β-cell failure — with structured escalation criteria and digital data capture.

Central to the programme is a dedicated MHOP Health Hub — a clinical space where patients are comprehensively assessed and offered the full range of interventions suited to their individual clinical needs, delivered by a specialist multidisciplinary team.

Clinical pathway

Three-stage glycaemic continuum

Each patient is assessed and managed according to their position on the glycaemic continuum, with structured escalation triggers and defined clinical outcomes at every stage.

Stage A

Prediabetes / NDH

Non-diabetic hyperglycaemia with preserved endogenous insulin secretion. Focus on metabolic optimisation, lifestyle and behavioural intervention, and prevention of progression to T2DM.

HbA1c range42–47 mmol/mol
TIR target≥ 70%
Primary goalRemission or stabilisation
Stage B

Established T2DM

Confirmed Type 2 Diabetes with progressive insulin resistance. Structured pharmacological, lifestyle and behavioural management with complication monitoring.

HbA1c range> 48 mmol/mol
TIR target≥ 70%
Primary goalControl and preservation
Stage C

Advanced β-cell failure

Long-standing T2DM with severe β-cell failure and insulin dependence. Advanced therapies including cell-based and regenerative approaches considered where clinically appropriate.

HbA1c range> 48 mmol/mol
Key markerC-peptide / HOMA-β
Primary goalStabilisation & β-cell support
MHOP Health Hub

A dedicated metabolic health studio

The MHOP Health Hub is a dedicated clinical space at Diree Godana Teaching Hospital where patients are comprehensively assessed and offered a personalised range of interventions matched to their clinical stage and individual needs. The Hub brings together four specialist disciplines — dietetics, diabetes nursing, physical activity, and behavioural therapy — supported by a comprehensive suite of clinical equipment.

The multidisciplinary team
🥗

Dietician

Nutrition & metabolic optimisation

Delivers individualised nutritional assessment and therapeutic dietary intervention. Supports weight management, glycaemic control through diet, and sustainable lifestyle change across all three clinical stages.

💉

Diabetes Specialist Nurse

Clinical care & medication management

Provides expert clinical assessment, medication review, CGM interpretation, complication screening, and patient education. Acts as the primary clinical coordinator across the glycaemic continuum.

🏃

Physical Activity Specialist

Exercise prescription & rehabilitation

Designs and delivers personalised exercise and physical rehabilitation programmes. Addresses sedentary behaviour, supports muscle mass preservation, and improves insulin sensitivity and cardiovascular risk profile.

🧠

Behavioural Therapist

Behaviour change & psychological support

Provides structured behavioural therapy to support lasting lifestyle change, medication adherence, self-management, and psychological wellbeing. Addresses the behavioural determinants of metabolic health including eating behaviour, sedentary habits, sleep and stress.

Equipment & capabilities

MHOP Health Hub Studio

A comprehensive suite of clinical equipment supporting assessment and intervention across every domain of the MHOP protocol.

Assessment equipment
Body composition analyser (BIA)

Weight, BMI, skeletal muscle mass, visceral fat, body fat %, total body water and basal metabolic rate.

Continuous Glucose Monitor (CGM)

Real-time glycaemic monitoring — TIR, TAR, TBR, MAGE and CV% — integrated into the MHOP digital record.

Automated blood pressure monitor

Validated clinical-grade BP measurement for cardiovascular risk assessment.

Point-of-care HbA1c analyser

Rapid in-clinic HbA1c enabling same-appointment decision-making.

Foot assessment kit

Neuropathy testing, vascular assessment and foot risk stratification.

Intervention & monitoring
Resistance training equipment

Free weights, resistance bands and functional training tools.

Aerobic exercise stations

Stationary cycle and step platforms for supervised aerobic exercise.

Dietary assessment tools

Food frequency questionnaires and nutritional counselling materials in three languages.

Behavioural therapy resources

Motivational interviewing guides, self-monitoring diaries, goal-setting frameworks and psychological screening instruments.

Patient education resources

Structured education materials in Afaan Oromo, Amharic and English.

Patients are offered a comprehensive initial assessment on their first visit, followed by a personalised care plan developed jointly with the multidisciplinary team.

Research & outcomes

Clinical outcomes and publications

Programme outcomes — published through formal research channels

The MHOP programme collects structured longitudinal clinical data across all enrolled patients. In keeping with patient confidentiality and research governance standards, individual or cohort clinical data is not published on this website.

Outcomes data will be disseminated through peer-reviewed publications, conference presentations, and formal research collaboration agreements.
